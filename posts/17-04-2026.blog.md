# Exploring Problem Space
At this stage of the project, I'm currently deciding on different community spaces and possible problems that could become key features for a web app. I am trying to think of the brief as more than just making a website that looks interesting. The concept needs to support a community, have clear functions that users would actually return to, and eventually be possible to build as a web app prototype.

Because of this, I started by looking at problem spaces where people might benefit from finding others in similar situations. The two ideas I personally considered first were a university study partner finder and an artist job finding agent.


## University Study Partner Finder
University Students: First year university students often report the new learning structure and environment being overwhelming, leading to students falling behind and feeling discouraged early on. One possible web app idea is a system for struggling students to find study partners online to help aid in the learning process of higher education.

Within this web app, users could set up profiles with their study subjects, academic timeline, and possibly a bio for more detailed information about study methods and personality. There could also be inner communities for specific degrees, making discovery more specific. Users may be able to chat and set up study sessions online or in-person.

The data for this idea could include users, subjects, study groups, posts, messages and scheduled sessions. However, the social aspects are similar to Reddit's sub-communities or existing class group chats, and its effectiveness could depend on having a larger starting sample size.


## Artist Job Finding Agent
Artists: The art industry is full of helpful individuals ready to reward people with talent, but people still find trouble finding entry level positions to start out and establish their career. My idea is to create a hub where artists looking to start their career can get introduced to established people and opportunities.

Specific functions within this web app can be a matching system where employers can see appropriate profiles for their hiring positions, while artists first configure profiles with their art mediums, style, experience, etc. Posts may also have a recommend button for employed artists endorsing unemployed artists to existing positions.

Some limitations or risks of this concept is that it proposes functions across different stakeholders, being employers and artists. Another limitation is that it may be difficult to gain trust from established companies to opt in as employers.

## What I Am Looking For In A Concept

As criteria for selecting the right idea, I want the concept to have a clear target audience, a comprehensive user flow, feasible database structures, and a balance between web-exclusive features and community integration.

## Initial Risks and Unknowns

At this point, I have not committed to one idea yet. The main uncertainty is which concept has the strongest balance between valuable functions, technical feasibility and manageable scope. The study partner idea has a clear student need, but it could become too broad if it includes profiles, forums, messaging, scheduling and reminders all at once. The artist job idea has a more unique career angle, but it depends on trust between different stakeholder interactions, while possessing the potential to be too complex in user flow.

To further develop incoming ideas, I need to once more consider data and logistics in implementing required functions, and whether they serve the purpose of the assessment brief.
