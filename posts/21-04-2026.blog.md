# Comparing Possible Concepts
Me and my group have been able to gather our individual ideas and are discussing them to later on commit to a singular idea. The ideas include a study group finder for first year university students, a arts career facilitator hub, a guide platform for gym beginners, and a recovery tracker for physical injuries. To judge on the feasibility of each design to be come a full prototype, we considered possible databases, user actions, as well as risks

As we brought each idea to compare, I was able to find both positives and negatives among them that deepened my understanding of the design goal.

## Standout and Reasoning
Through consideration of both technical and conceptual scope, the recovery hub idea stood out due to its straightforward interaction flow of users setting recovery context and recording progress, then gaining insights from past experiences of other users. The main dealbreakers for the other ideas were that:
- The study group finder featured complex messaging and scheduling features, as well as higher institution involvement that conflicted with the logistics of developing the design.
- The artist career hub depended on external job data, as well as it didn't have a clear way to verify job quality.
- The fitness guide was sound but personalised plans were too complex, with chance that exercise database becoming too large. 

### Design Trade-Off
The recovery hub design however is not free of risks, the medical feedback features of the app made it so that it would need to avoid giving direct medical advice, and medical information that can be found through discussion posts can be sensitive and requires proper privacy policies. Therefore to execute the design, we would need to treat it as a reflective forum for people with diverse health experiences and a place of community support for the ill/injured rather than diagnosis.

## Forward Direction
The comparison of data needs and core functions helped identified the idea with the best balance between technical feasibility, interesting topics, and assessnment aligned purposes. I will look further into defining the concept clearly and map out the core requirements of the design, with possible data requirements while assessing presented risks.
